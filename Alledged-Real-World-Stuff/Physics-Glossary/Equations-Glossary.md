#### Newton's Laws of motion

[Wikipedia: Newton Laws of Motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton's_laws_of_motion): ****Newton's laws of motion** are three [physical laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_laws "Physical laws") that describe the relationship between the [motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion "Motion") of an object and the [forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Force "Force") acting on it. These laws, which provide the basis for **Newtonian mechanics**, can be paraphrased as follows:
>1. A body remains at rest, or in motion at a constant speed in a straight line, unless it is acted upon by a force.
>2. At any instant of time, the net force on a body is equal to the body's [acceleration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acceleration "Acceleration") multiplied by its [mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass "Mass") or, equivalently, the rate at which the body's [momentum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Momentum "Momentum") is changing with time.
>3. If two bodies exert forces on each other, these forces have the same magnitude but opposite directions

The cause and effect equation: $F = ma$ can be written as $a = \frac{f}{m}$; [YouTube: 99% of physics explained in 5 equations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OKBapkJbB8c), because external things can cause things to move.
- **Force (F):** Think of this as the "push" or "pull." It’s the effort required to change what an object is doing.
- **Mass (m):** This is the "stuff" an object is made of—its resistance to being moved (inertia).
- **Acceleration (a):** This is how quickly the object picks up speed or changes direction.
